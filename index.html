For Travelling Guests

Getting Around Armenia

Arriving

Fly into Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) in Yerevan. Many nationalities enter Armenia visa-free for up to 180 days.

Heading south

Goris and Sisian are about a 3.5–4 hour drive south of Yerevan through Syunik. We will arrange shuttles between the wedding events.

Reaching Tatev

Tatev Monastery is reached by the Wings of Tatev — the world's longest reversible cableway — soaring above the Vorotan gorge.

Where to stay

Stay in central Yerevan for the 4th, then in Goris or near Sisian for the 6th and 7th. A recommended list of hotels will follow.

Good to Know

Questions & Answers

Where do the celebrations take place?

Across three locations: the welcome party in Yerevan (4 July), the ritual celebration in Goris (6 July), and the wedding in the Sisian & Tatev area of Syunik (7 July). Follow the journey map above for the full route.

How do I travel between the cities?

Syunik is roughly a 3.5–4 hour drive south of Yerevan. We will organise shuttle transport between the events; details will follow with your invitation.

What is Vardavar — and will I get wet?

Vardavar is a beloved Armenian summer festival where people joyfully douse one another with water. Our welcome party embraces it — so wear something easy-going and be ready for a friendly splash!

What should I wear?

Garden formal in our colours of burgundy, olive and cream. As Tatev and Goris involve ancient stone and open ground, we suggest comfortable shoes for the wedding day.

A note on gifts

Your presence — travelling all this way to be with us in Armenia — is truly the greatest gift. Should you wish to give something more, a note will follow closer to the day.
